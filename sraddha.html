Śraddhā

The meeting point of knowledge and devotion.

Meaning of Śraddhā

Śraddhā is not blind faith. It is a steady, reverent readiness to inquire — a sincere orientation of heart and mind toward truth. It holds doubt lightly, acts with sincerity, and allows the seeker to walk a path that honours both reason and receptivity.

In practice, Śraddhā is the calm courage to learn, to test, and to transform one’s life with wisdom.

The Role of Śraddhā

Knowledge without Śraddhā can become sterile; devotion without knowledge can become sentimental. Śraddhā unites the two: it anchors inquiry in humility and gives devotion a compass. When we cultivate Śraddhā, our studies are not merely academic — they become acts of service toward a wiser life.

Main Motto

“May our knowledge be guided by humility, our intellect by compassion, and our reason by devotion to truth.”
“श्रद्धा ज्ञानस्य मूलम् — Śraddhā is the root of knowledge.”

Symbol

A symbolic mandala or lamp may be placed here to remind the seeker of inner light and steady attention.
